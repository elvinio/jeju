Gotchas & things to sort out

Grouped by when they matter — documents to sort before you fly, driving quirks, weather and packing, money, school-term logistics, and a few notes specific to the slower two-base plan.

Entry & documents

Do first

International Driving Permit (IDP) — get it before you fly

Self-driving in Korea requires a 1949 Geneva Convention IDP alongside your passport and Singapore driving licence — all three, physical originals, at the rental counter. Digital copies and photos are not accepted.

Apply in person at AA Singapore (they're the only authorised issuer) — it cannot be issued in Korea, couriered internationally, or emailed. Get one for each parent who plans to drive, ~S$25 each; bring a passport photo and your licence.

Good news

No visa, no K-ETA

Singapore passport holders are exempt from Korea's K-ETA/visa requirement (extended through end of 2026), and flying direct into Jeju also qualifies for Jeju's own 30-day visa-free entry. Either way, just bring passports — no advance application needed.

Note

The direct-entry rule only applies if you fly straight to Jeju

If you ever add a Seoul/Busan leg to this trip, entering the mainland first changes the entry basis (standard 90-day Korea visa-free rules apply instead of Jeju's separate waiver) — not relevant for this itinerary since it's a direct SIN–CJU round trip, but worth knowing if plans change.

The two-base plan

Good news

You only unpack twice

Both routes stay four nights in the north, then four on one coast — a single change of base for the whole trip. Book both stays as early as you can; all-resort family rooms with pool access are the first things to sell out in November, and you're committing to specific properties rather than swapping pensions on the fly.

Important

Your last night isn't in Jeju City — leave margin on departure day

To keep it to one move, the final night is on your chosen coast, not back near the airport. On the East route that's a ~1-hour drive to CJU on the morning of departure; on the West route it's only ~40 min. Fine for a mid-morning or later flight — just don't book the earliest departure of the day, and refuel the evening before to keep the airport run short.

Driving in Jeju

Important

Google Maps won't give driving directions in Korea

A Korean data-export law blocks Google's turn-by-turn navigation nationwide, Jeju included. Install Naver Map (best all-rounder, has an English mode) or KakaoMap before you leave, and do a quick test search at home so you're not figuring it out at the rental counter.

Reserve ahead

Booster seats need requesting in advance

Korean law requires child restraints for under-6s; boosters aren't legally mandatory at 7 and 9 but are still the safer call and rental companies stock limited numbers. Request 2 boosters when you book the car, not on arrival — expect ~S$1–3/day each.

Good news

No tolls anywhere on Jeju

There are no expressways on the island, so no Hi-pass transponder or toll fees to worry about — just ordinary coastal and country roads. Speed cameras and school-zone limits (30km/h) are strictly enforced though, and fines apply even to rental cars.

East route

Udo Island doesn't take your rental car

On the North & East plan, rental cars can't be ferried over for a day trip — only if you're staying on Udo overnight. Park at the Seongsan Port lot (cheap, ~S$4/day) and rent an e-bike or cart once you're on the island instead.

Note

Refuel before returning the car

Standard "return full" policy — refuel near the airport (or on the way in) before drop-off to avoid the rental company's inflated top-up rate. Stick to a petrol or hybrid compact SUV rather than an EV; Jeju pushes EV rentals hard, but charging-stop logistics add friction you don't need with two young kids in the car.

Weather & packing

Good news

November is Jeju's driest, mildest month

Expect 13–17°C by day, ~12°C overnight, and only ~74mm of rain spread over the month — genuinely pleasant self-drive weather, and ideal for the forests, craters and coastal walks this plan leans on. Pack layers and a windproof jacket; coastal wind can be sharp even on a mild day.

Note

Short days

Sunrise is around 07:20 and sunset around 17:25 in late November. That suits this slower plan — late starts, and outdoor plans (the Saebyeol or Yongnuni oreum at sunset, say) wrapping by dusk rather than being squeezed into a packed day.

Note

Sea's too cold to swim, pools are heated

Pack swimsuits anyway — every base on this plan is a resort with a heated indoor pool, which is very much in play even though the ocean itself is off the table this time of year (the turquoise beaches are for paddling and sandcastles, not swimming). Hallasan's summit trail can see early snow/ice in November and isn't included — it's not a good match for a 7- and 9-year-old regardless, though a gentle lower-forest trail is an easy add if you want one.

Money & connectivity

Note

Cards over cash, but carry some won

Korea is highly card-friendly — Visa/Mastercard work almost everywhere. Keep some cash for markets, small farm stalls, and parking. Tipping isn't expected anywhere, including restaurants and drivers.

Good news

Convenience stores are everywhere

CU, GS25 and 7-Eleven are on almost every corner — handy for a cheap breakfast, snacks, or anything you forgot to pack, especially useful travelling with kids.

School term & timing

Good news

Nov 21 lines up with the school year ending

MOE's 2026 school year for primary schools ends Friday 20 November — the day before this trip starts — so a leave-of-absence application likely isn't needed if your children are in mainstream MOE primary school. Double-check your specific school's calendar for any final-week events, and check separately if either child is in an international school, since those calendars differ.

Practical odds and ends

Note

The direct Singapore–Jeju flight route is fairly new

T'way Air (and Scoot) only started flying this nonstop route from August 2025. It should be well-established by November 2026, but it's worth reconfirming the schedule a bit closer to the date rather than assuming it's as fixed as a long-running route.

Note

Restaurant quirks

Many black pork BBQ places have a minimum order of 2 portions per table, and small local restaurants often close one fixed day a week plus a mid-afternoon break — check hours before making a special trip. Booking a resort with breakfast included takes the pressure off the mornings.

Note

Pack basic kids' medicine

Pharmacies are common but not on every corner outside town centres. Some coastal and oreum-access roads wind enough to trigger motion sickness — worth having the usual on hand.
