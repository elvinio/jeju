Day by day

9 days, one clockwise loop

Sunrise (~07:20) and sunset (~17:25) are both early in late November, so mornings start a little earlier than usual and outdoor plans wrap by dusk. Total driving over the trip is roughly 350–400km — no expressways on Jeju, so it's all coastal and country roads.

21
Sat
Jeju City
Arrival
Fly
Singapore Changi → Jeju Int'l (CJU), direct, ~6h. Likely an afternoon/evening arrival.
On landing
Clear immigration under Jeju's direct-entry visa waiver (no visa/K-ETA needed for Singapore passports). Collect the rental car — bring IDP, passport and SG licence; confirm the 2 booster seats are fitted.
Evening
Check into a Jeju City hotel. Easy dinner nearby — Dongmun Market or a black pork restaurant. Early night to recover from the flight.
Base: Jeju City

East
Into the East — caves & coastline
Morning
Drive to Gimnyeong (~40 min). Manjanggul Cave — a lava tube, a constant ~11–12°C inside, so bring a light jacket even though it's mild outside.
Afternoon
Seopjikoji coastal walk, then Aqua Planet Jeju aquarium next door — a reliable indoor option if the weather turns.
Evening
Check into a Seongsan-area family pension. Dinner in Seongsan village.

→ ~50–60 min driving today

Base: Seongsan (East), night 1 of 2
23
Mon
East
Sunrise Peak & Udo Island
Early AM
Climb Seongsan Ilchulbong (Sunrise Peak) — 30–45 min moderate trail up a tuff-cone crater rim. Go early to beat the tour buses.
Day trip
Ferry from Seongsan Port to Udo Island (leave the rental car in the port car park — cars can't be taken across for a day visit). Rent an e-bike or family cart to circle the island: Seobin Baeksa beach, Sanho beach, the famous peanut ice cream.
Evening
Last ferry back is around 17:30. Dinner in Seongsan.
Base: Seongsan (East), night 2 of 2

East to South, via the old villages
Morning
Drive toward Jungmun via the south coast road (~1–1.5h with a stop).
En route
Seongeup Folk Village — a preserved walled village with thatched houses and stone walls — or Pyoseon Whitesand Beach, depending on the weather.

Check into the Jungmun resort. Let the kids loose on the pool/kids' facilities after two travel-heavy days.

South
Seogwipo highlights
Morning
Cheonjiyeon Waterfall (easy paved path) and Jusangjeolli Cliff (hexagonal basalt columns — striking, and a 10-minute walk).
Afternoon
Teddy Bear Museum or a trick-art museum as a rainy-day-proof option; Oedolgae Rock coastal walk or Jungmun Saekdal Beach if it's clear.

Back to the resort for the pool and an easy dinner — this is the built-in rest day.
Base: Jungmun / Seogwipo (South), night 2 of 2

West
South to West, via Andeok
Morning
O'sulloc Tea Museum and Innisfree Jeju House (both free entry, side by side, working tea fields) — then Camellia Hill next door, in full bloom this time of year (camellias flower Nov–Mar).
Afternoon
Continue west, optionally via the Sanbangsan / Yongmeori coast viewpoint, to Aewol. Check into a pension or small hotel.
Evening
Sunset stroll along Aewol's cafe street.

→ ~70–90 min driving today

Base: Aewol / Hallim (West), night 1 of 2
27
Fri
West
West coast day
Morning
Hallim Park — botanical garden, caves and a folk village in one, right next to Hyeopjae Beach.
Afternoon
Mandarin-picking at a local farm — peak season, and a reliable hit with kids. Handam coastal walk near Aewol afterwards: flat, stroller-friendly, tide pools.
Evening
Saebyeol Oreum — a short, easy climb and one of the island's best sunset spots.
