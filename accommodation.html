Where to stay

Four bases, one splurge

Since lodging is flexible, the plan mixes family pensions (cheap, spacious, usually have a kitchenette) with two nights at a proper resort in the middle of the trip. Named properties below are representative of each tier — treat them as a shortlist to compare, not a booking recommendation, since rates and availability need confirming closer to the date.

Jeju City 2 nights total · Sat 21 (arrival) + Sat 28 (departure)
Mid-range hotel
~S$115 / night · S$230 total

Close to the airport, useful on a late arrival and an early departure day. Plenty of dinner options nearby (Dongmun Market, black pork street) so you're not searching for food after a 6-hour flight.

  • Ramada Plaza Jeju — centrally located business hotel, family rooms available.
  • L7 Jeju by Lotte — newer mid-range option near the old town.
  • A family-run guesthouse near Yongduam / the airport, for a cheaper option with similar convenience.
Seongsan · East 2 nights · Sun 22 – Mon 23
Family pension
~S$100 / night · S$200 total

Small, family-run pensions dominate this area — usually an ondol (heated-floor) room sleeping 4, sometimes with a small kitchenette, a short drive from both Seongsan Ilchulbong and the Udo ferry terminal.

  • Family pensions in Seongsan-eup near Gwangchigi Beach — walkable to the sunrise-peak trailhead.
  • Guesthouses closer to the ferry terminal if you want to walk to the first sailing.
Jungmun · South 2 nights · Tue 24 – Wed 25
Resort splurge
~S$450 / night · S$900 total

This is the one nice stay — a proper family resort with a heated indoor/outdoor pool, which matters in November. Book this leg first and earliest, since resort family rooms sell out before pensions do.

  • Kensington Jeju Jungmun — family rooms, pool, walking distance to Jungmun Beach.
  • Grand Josun Jeju or Haevichi Hotel & Resort — higher tier if the budget stretches (see the buffer on the Budget page).
  • Ramada Plaza Jeju Seogwipo as a slightly more affordable 4-star alternative.
Aewol · West 2 nights · Thu 26 – Fri 27
Family pension / small hotel
~S$110 / night · S$220 total

Aewol and neighbouring Hallim have a good spread of boutique pensions, many within walking distance of the cafe street and the coastal walk — handy for an easy last full day before heading back to Jeju City.

  • Pensions along the Aewol coastline, most with sea-view rooms.
  • Hallim-side options if you'd rather be closer to Hallim Park and Hyeopjae Beach.
