Where to stay

Two stays, both a splurge

Every night is a proper hotel or resort — no pensions this time. Each route uses just two bases, four nights apiece, so there's a single check-in, a single move, and a heated pool to come home to after every day out (the sea's too cold to swim in November). The first base is shared by both routes; you only pick the second. Named properties are a representative shortlist to compare, not a booking recommendation — confirm live rates and family-room availability closer to the date.

North base · both routes Sat 21 – Tue 24 · 4 nights

Jeju City & north coast

10–20 min from the airport
Resort / upscale hotel
~S$320 / night · S$1,280 for 4 nights

Close to the airport for an easy arrival, and the best base for the northern forests, the Sangumburi crater and the Manjanggul coast. Look for a resort with a heated indoor pool so the rest-day afternoons have somewhere to land.

  • Grand Hyatt Jeju — large family rooms, big heated pools and an on-site aquarium; the most resort-like option in the city.
  • Lotte City Hotel Jeju or Maison Glad Jeju — reliable upscale hotels with family rooms, a notch below on price.
  • A sea-view resort along the Jocheon / Hamdeok north-east coast if you'd rather wake up to water than the city.
East base · Option A Wed 25 – Sat 28 · 4 nights

Seongsan & the east coast

for the North & East route
Resort / upscale hotel
~S$260 / night · S$1,040 for 4 nights

Walk-out access to Sunrise Peak, the Udo ferry and the Seopjikoji coast. Book a room with the pool and, ideally, an Ilchulbong or sea view for the down-time afternoons.

  • Haevichi Hotel & Resort Jeju — the big full-service resort on this coast (Pyoseon), with pools and family rooms; a short drive south of Seongsan.
  • Lava Hotel or Grabel Hotel Seongsan — newer design hotels right by Sunrise Peak.
  • Playce Camp Jeju — a large, playful family-friendly complex beside Seongsan with easy dining on site.
West base · Option B Wed 25 – Sat 28 · 4 nights

Aewol / Hallim & the west coast

for the North & West route
Resort / upscale hotel
~S$260 / night · S$1,040 for 4 nights

Sea-view stays within reach of Hyeopjae's turquoise beaches, Hallim Park and the sunset oreums. This coast leans boutique rather than mega-resort, so prioritise a heated pool and a family room.

  • Bayhills Hotel & Resort or a sea-view boutique hotel along the Aewol coastline — walkable to the café street and Handam walk.
  • Hallim-side hotels near Hyeopjae Beach if you'd rather be next to the sand and Hallim Park.
  • Jeju Shinhwa World (Marriott / Somerset) — a larger resort option a little inland to the south-west, if you want full resort facilities and don't mind a slightly longer run to the beaches.
