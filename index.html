Family trip plan · two ways

Jeju, slow and green

Two unhurried, nature-first itineraries for 2 adults and 2 children (7 & 9), built around a direct Singapore–Jeju flight and one rental car. Each version stays in just two places — both a hotel or resort — so there's a single move mid-trip and nothing else to pack and unpack. We skip the busy south entirely and trade the clockwise dash for long mornings, forests, beaches and grassy volcanic craters.

Pick a half of the island

Option A opens with four unhurried nights in the north, near the airport, then commits to the eastern coast. Option B flips that order — west first, north last — because Scoot's only Jeju→Singapore flights currently depart CJU at 08:35 and 10:05: there's no later option, so ending close to the airport matters more than starting there. See Gotchas for the flight-timing note. Choose the eastern half for sunrise peaks and island-hopping, or the western half for tea fields, long turquoise beaches and sunset oreums. You only need one.

Why slower works here

  • Two bases A single mid-trip move means one check-in, one check-out, and no living out of a suitcase. Kids settle; you actually use the pool.
  • Short hops Nothing on either plan is more than about an hour's drive, and most days are 20–40 minutes at most. Late starts are the default.
  • Nature over tickets Forests, beaches, craters and oreums — many free or a few dollars — carry the trip, with a couple of paid highlights per base rather than a packed checklist.
  • Built-in rest Every base has at least one day with nothing scheduled but the resort, a beach and an early dinner.

Before you fly

Do first

Get a 1949-convention International Driving Permit

Apply in person at AA Singapore before you leave — it can't be issued in Korea or emailed. Bring it plus your passport and Singapore licence to the rental counter. See Gotchas.

Good news

No visa, no K-ETA, no leave-of-absence

Singapore passports are K-ETA/visa-exempt for Korea, and Nov 21 is the day after MOE's 2026 school year ends (Fri 20 Nov) — this trip shouldn't need a leave-of-absence application.
