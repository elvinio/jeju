Option B · Day by day

West & North, unhurried

Four nights on the west coast first, then four back in the green north — a single move, both stays a hotel or resort. The order is flipped from a simple clockwise loop on purpose: Scoot's only Jeju→Singapore flights currently depart CJU at 08:35 and 10:05 (see Gotchas), so the last night is worth spending 10–20 min from the airport rather than 40. The pace itself is still deliberately loose: long turquoise beaches, working tea fields, camellias in full bloom and one of Jeju's best sunset oreums, then forests and a grassy crater to finish. Nothing here is more than about an hour's drive. Late November days are short (sunrise ~07:20, sunset ~17:25), so plans wrap by dusk and dinners are early.

Singapore Changi → Jeju Int'l (CJU), direct, ~6h. A Saturday afternoon/evening arrival on Scoot's day flight, landing around 18:35.

Clear immigration under Jeju's direct-entry visa waiver (no visa/K-ETA needed for Singapore passports). Evening
Check in and stay put. Easy dinner nearby — Aewol's café street or a seafood restaurant — then an early night to recover from the flight.

A slow resort breakfast, then drive inland (~30–40 min).

Saryeoni Forest Trail or Bijarim Forest — flat, soft-floored walks through towering cedar and nutmeg trees. Stroller-and-kid friendly, and about as restorative as walking gets.
